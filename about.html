Our Heritage

The Story

In 1903, inside a 10 × 15-foot wooden shed, William S. Harley and Arthur Davidson hand-built their first motorized bicycle. What started as youthful ambition became an American revolution on two wheels.

Through two World Wars, the Great Depression, and every cultural shift since, Harley-Davidson endured — not by following trends, but by defining them. Our V-twin rumble became the soundtrack of freedom itself.

The Mission

We exist to fulfill dreams of personal freedom. Every machine we build carries the DNA of over a century of relentless innovation and an uncompromising commitment to the rider experience.

From Milwaukee assembly lines to highways spanning every continent, our community of riders share one truth: the road doesn't just take you somewhere — it makes you someone.
